Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Shopify Scripts and Shopify Functions?
Shopify Scripts are written in Ruby and executed on Shopify's servers, but are being deprecated. Shopify Functions are their modern replacement, allowing developers to inject custom logic into Shopify's backend using WebAssembly (Wasm) for discounts, shipping, and payment customizations with better performance and scalability.
Do Shopify Scripts still work after June 30, 2026?
No. After June 30, 2026, existing Shopify Scripts will completely stop functioning. Shopify Plus teams must migrate to Shopify Functions before this sunset date to avoid checkout and store disruptions.
Is DeckOS HQ a Shopify app or a downloadable toolkit?
DeckOS HQ is a downloadable migration toolkit, not a Shopify app. It provides the strategic and operational framework (playbooks, trackers, QA matrices) for your team to plan and execute the migration.
What should Shopify Plus teams audit before migrating from Scripts?
Teams must inventory all active and inactive Scripts, document their exact checkout behavior, determine their business risk (P1-P4), and decide whether to replace them with native features, third-party apps, or custom-built Shopify Functions.
Can agencies use this across multiple Shopify Plus clients?
Yes. For multi-client agency use, choose the PLUS / AGENCY version, which includes the appropriate usage rights for your team to use the framework across multiple client migration projects.
Does this include implementation services?
No. This toolkit is designed for teams managing the transition internally or with their existing agency partners. It is an operational framework, not a done-for-you implementation service.
What exactly is included in the toolkit?
The bundle includes the 46-slide strategic playbook, a ready-to-use execution tracker in Google Sheets, a printable QA testing matrix, the Weekly Triage guide, plus developer-ready boilerplates and an AI prompt library.
How do teams typically migrate from Scripts to Functions?
Most teams need to inventory existing Scripts, prioritize by business risk, choose replacement paths, validate with QA, and roll out with rollback readiness. This toolkit is built around that workflow.
